Is computer organization and architecture useful for a placement?

It really depends on the companies you are interviewing or aiming to interview for. As far as my limited knowledge about campus placements goes, most companies do not expect a lot of specialized knowledge about subjects like computer architecture. They are keen on probing you on mathematics and programming. Especially web-development, e-commerce organizations don’t really care about specialized technical knowledge. Data Structure and Algorithms are their bread and butter. A few hard-core technical companies and design houses though might go a level deeper. These include the likes of Nvidia, Intel, Samsung, Qualcomm and some EDA companies (Synopsys, Cadence) etc. Since these companies are in a niche domain of chip-designing, you’ll definitely stand an advantage by knocking off a few questions about computer organization and architecture.

What is an interrupt in the computer organization and architecture?

Interrupt signal is a active low (0) or active high (1) signal used to send to a device to say "Hey, stop doing what you're doing and listen to me. I've got something for you". Now it's up to the interrupt receiver device. It may stop it's operations and start communicating with interrupting device or it may reject the interruption request. Some interrupts can be overridden, those interrupts are called maskable interrupts. And some interrupts can't be overridden no matter what operations are going on, device is bound to accept the interrupt and stop doing operations and listen to interrupting device. This kind of interrupts are called non maskable interrupts.

Is computer organization and architecture an important subject? Do we need to study it in depth?

Honestly it is probably not a study area that will get you many jobs today. If you pursue anything related to code performance or tuning, then such study is very important. But not many jobs are offered in those areas.
Obtaining functionality, using big data methods, using neural networks, are all more job friendly studies today.
